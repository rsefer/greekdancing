Kalamatianós

From Wikipedia: The Kalamatianós (Greek: Καλαματιανός) is one of the best known dances of Greece. As is the case with most Greek folk dances, it is danced in chain with a counterclockwise rotation, the dancers holding hands. There are 12 steps in the dance corresponding to 4 bars of music. These steps include 10 steps counterclockwise ("forward") followed by 2 steps clockwise ("backwards").
